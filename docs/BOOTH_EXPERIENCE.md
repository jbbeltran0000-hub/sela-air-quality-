# Part 2: The Booth Experience (Operations)

## The 'How' – Operational Setup

This section describes how to set up and run the **"Voice to Action Clinic"** at community events. It is designed for **maximum bilingual engagement** and high-volume data capture in a 3-hour shift.

---

## Station Layout (Recommended for 3-hour event)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         BANNER: "USE YOUR VOICE / USA TU VOZ"   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  Station 1                Station 2              Station 3
  [FLIR Visual Proof]     [4Ws Voice Clinic]    [Live Map Pulse]
  Thermal Camera          Tablet Form           Large Screen
  "See the toxicity"      "Tell your story"     "Watch it verify"

   Staff: 1-2             Staff: 1-2             Staff: 1
   Shift: 3 hours         Shift: 3 hours        Shift: 3 hours
   Bilingual training     Bilingual training    Bilingual training
```

---

## Station 1: FLIR Visual Proof

### Objective
Residents must **see** the toxicity before they believe the system. Thermal imaging makes the invisible visible.

### Equipment
- FLIR thermal camera (e.g., FLIR E5, E8, or smartphone attachment)
- iPad/tablet displaying live thermal feed
- Label printed: **"WHAT THE SENSORS MISS"**
- Optional: Scale model truck or propane heater to demonstrate heat signatures

### Setup
1. Position FLIR camera on tripod at 45° angle to capture high-traffic area or nearby exhaust source.
2. Connect camera to tablet via USB or WiFi.
3. Display live thermal feed on tablet screen.
4. Place signage: *"Look at the colors. Red and yellow = heat. Blue = cold. Your lungs feel that heat."*

### Staff Script (Bilingual)

**English:**
> "Look at this thermal camera. The sensor on that pole over there says the air is 'good.' But look at the camera—do you see that red plume? That's heat from a truck exhaust. The sensor misses that. We don't. That's why YOUR eyes matter. That's why we're here."

**Spanish:**
> "Mira esta cámara térmica. El sensor en ese poste dice que el aire es 'bueno.' Pero mira aquí—¿ves esa pluma roja? Es calor del escape de un camión. El sensor no lo ve. Nosotros sí. Por eso TUS ojos importan. Por eso estamos aquí."

## Station 2: The '4 Ws' Voice Clinic

### Objective
Capture **high-fidelity testimony in 30 seconds** using an emoji-based, mobile-first form.

### Equipment
- iPad or Android tablet running secured web form
- Form interface: Emoji-based icons for quick selection
- Haptic feedback (phone vibration) on form submission
- QR code for residents to self-report from their phones

### Staff Script

**English:**
> "Your story is our data. If you smell something or see something, use your voice. You don't need to be a scientist. You just need to tell us: What? Where? When? Who? We will validate it for you."

**Spanish:**
> "Tu historia es nuestro dato. Si hueles algo o ves algo, usa tu voz. No necesitas ser un científico. Solo cuéntanos: ¿Qué? ¿Dónde? ¿Cuándo? ¿Quién? Lo verificaremos por ti."

---

## Station 3: Live Pulse Map (The 'Aha!' Hook)

### Objective
Close the loop **immediately**. Show residents their data is being processed in real-time.

### Equipment
- Large monitor (42"-55") mounted at eye level
- Live map dashboard
- Real-time WebSocket connection to backend verification system
- Audio notification: Soft "ping" sound when new reports arrive

### Staff Script

**English:**
> "Watch this map. Your report just went in. See it pulsing? It's asking the nearest sensor—right there—'Did you see what they saw?' When you and the sensor match, it becomes a Verified Hotspot. Red means we found it. That means action."

**Spanish:**
> "Mira este mapa. Tu reporte acaba de entrar. ¿Lo ves pulsando? Le está preguntando al sensor más cercano: '¿Viste lo que ellos vieron?' Cuando tú y el sensor coinciden, se convierte en un Punto Crítico Verificado. Rojo significa que lo encontramos. Eso significa acción."

## Staff Training & Protocols

### Bilingual Competency
- All staff wear **"DE LA VOZ A LA ACCIÓN"** (From Voice to Action) t-shirts
- Minimum 2-hour training on all three stations
- Role-play scenarios: skeptical resident, non-English speaker, person with health condition

### Data Privacy & Consent
- **Privacy Script:** "We will share your validated data with air quality agencies. Your name will be anonymous in the public ledger. Is that okay?"
- Consent form (printed + digital) signed before form submission
- HIPAA-compliant if health symptoms are reported

### Shift Recommendations
- **Optimal:** 2-3 staff per station × 3-hour shifts = 6-9 total staff
- **Minimum:** 1 staff per station × 4-hour shift = 3 total staff (higher stress)
- **Peak Hours:** 11 AM - 3 PM (family-friendly window)
